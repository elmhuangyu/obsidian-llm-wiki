# Memex

**Memex** is a theoretical device first described by Vannevar Bush in his 1945 essay "As We May Think." It was conceived as an electromechanical desk that would allow an individual to store all their books, records, and communications, with the ability to create "associative trails" between items.

## Connection to Modern PKM

The Memex is often cited as a foundational vision for hypertext, the web, and modern [[Personal-Knowledge-Management|personal knowledge management (PKM)]] tools.

- **Visions and Realities**: While the web realized the scale of information, it initially lacked the private, actively curated "trails" envisioned by Bush.
- **LLM Wiki Connection**: Andrej Karpathy notes that the [[LLM-Wiki]] pattern is closer in spirit to the Memex than what the modern web became, with LLMs solving the critical problem of who maintains the system and builds the associations.
