# Personal Knowledge Management (PKM)

**Personal Knowledge Management (PKM)** refers to the set of processes, tools, and workflows individuals use to gather, classify, store, search, and retrieve knowledge for personal or professional growth.

## Evolutions in PKM

- **Digital Notebooks**: Transition from physical paper to digital tools like Evernote or OneNote.
- **Bi-directional Linking**: Tools like [[Obsidian]], Roam Research, and Logseq introduced non-linear, interlinked note-taking.
- **AI-Augmented PKM**: The integration of LLMs to assist in summarizing, synthesizing, and maintaining knowledge bases (e.g., the [[LLM-Wiki]] and [[Personal-Knowledge-Wiki]] patterns).
- **[[Self-Sovereign-AI]]**: A 2026 shift toward local-first, privacy-preserving AI systems where the user maintains absolute control over their data and the AI agents acting upon it.

## Historical Precedents

- **[[Memex]]**: Vannevar Bush's vision (1945) for a personal, curated knowledge store with associative "trails" between documents.
